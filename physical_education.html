This is a full-body beginner flexibility practice that can be adapted to almost any level of flexibility. It works from head to toe. It will take around 15 minutes. I'll include a link to the routine on YouTube as well as a PDF file where you can download it.

This routine includes neck side flexion, shoulder extension reaches, child pose, child pose with lat focus, child pose with pec twist, McKenzie push up, leg tuck up, leg side drops, cross body glute pull, hip swivels, squat to pike, low lunge and lunge with a twist in the left leg, frog pose rocks, low lunge, and lunge with a twist in the right leg this time, frog pose squat and ending with a squat.

3. To provide baseline data for selection of physical activities for enhancement of health and skill performance.

4. To gather data for the development of norms and standards.

5. To be motivated and guided in selecting sports for recreation, competition, and lifetime participation.
